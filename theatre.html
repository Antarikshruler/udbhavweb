THEATRE EVENT RULES

     1. Short Play
     Anshuman : 9964596569

  1. Scripts are to be sent to the Theatre co-ordinators on or before April 15th after which they will be screened a select few scripts will be shortlisted.
  2. Scene and set changes are allowed during the course of the play.
  3. The duration of the play shall not exceed 30 minutes.
  4. The time will be counted as soon as the signal is given to start the play. Empty stage to empty stage shall be followed strictly. For stage setting and removal of set and properties, up to 5 minutes will be given after taking charge of the stage.
  5. The number of participants should not exceed 10 and the maximum number of accompanists should not exceed 4. The participating team shall bring their own set/ stage paraphernalia, make-up materials, etc, light and general paraphernalia such as ordinary furniture which may be provided on advance information.
  6. Participants may speak in Hindi, English or any regional language of India. In case the language is regional one, synopsis of the play with translation in English or Hindi must be submitted to the theatre coordinator pre-screening AND on the day of the performance.
  7. Vulgarity or bitter insinuation in presentation should be avoided. Only innocent satire or humour is expected.
  8. The participating team must report to the in charge of the competition at least 2 hours before the presentation of the play.
  9. Judgment will be based on the qualities of the play like theme, work of acting, stage craft, design and general impression, etc.
  10. Decision of the panel of judges will be final and binding upon all.
  11. Accompanists will either speak from the background or will play upon musical instruments for background music.

      2. Short Movies
      Supreeth HS : 8904430345

  1. Time limit is 15 minutes from title to credits.
  2. The participant must submit their movie in CD/USB drive before 9 a.m. on the day of the event.
  3. There should be no objectionable content; portrayal of illicit substances, tobacco products, or alcoholic beverages is not advised. Any such portrayal must be accompanied with a statutory warning in readable text at the bottom of the screen. Participants who do not adhere to this rule will be disqualified.
  4. Documentaries are allowed, but must adhere to time limit and content restrictions as stated above.
  5. Names, phone numbers and USN of the students along with a synopsis of the film must be provided at the time of submission.

     3. Street Play
     Mani Bharathi: 9141447427

  1. Time limit is 20 minutes from start to end.
    2. Maximum number of participants is 15.
  2. Language of the play can be English, Hindi or any other regional language.
  3. Vulgarity or bitter insinuation in presentation should be avoided. Only innocent satire or humour is expected.
  4. The participating team shall bring their own paraphernalia, make-up materials, costumes etc. General paraphernalia such as ordinary furniture which may be provided on advance information.
  5. No lights or audio equipment will be provided (mics, amplifiers, etc.) Background music is allowed, but equipment must be brought by the team. Use of mic is strictly not allowed.
  6. Decision of judges will be final and binding for all teams.

 

       4. Mono Act/Mimicry
       Shreyas Somashekar: 9742707623

  1. There should only be one participant on the stage and accompanists are strictly not allowed.
  2. Participants can perform a piece of mono act/mimicry act in Hindi, english or a combination of both. Props are allowed. Music is also allowed.
  3. Performance must consist of a single act with no blackouts.
  4. The act should not exceed 8 minutes. Time Limit should be followed strictly. Participants will be penalized by judges for not doing so.
  5. The judging criteria include Script, Acting, Characterization, Delivery and Overall Impact
  6. Obscenity (at the discretion of the judges) of any kind is not allowed and will lead to immediate disqualification.
  7. Any kind of fluid or flame is not allowed on stage - must not set the stage on fire.
  8. The decision of the judges will be final and binding.
